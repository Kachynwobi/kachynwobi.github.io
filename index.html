Flagship Case Study

Problem → design decisions → outcomes

Flood Risk Visualization for Urban Decision-Making

Urban planners and community stakeholders needed a faster way to understand where flood risk intersects with vulnerable populations. I translated complex datasets into a map-based experience optimized for clarity, interpretability, and scale-aware exploration.

Context
  • Users: planners, policy teams, community stakeholders
  • Problem: fragmented + static outputs, hard to compare regions
  • Goal: legible at multiple scales, accessible interpretation
My role
  • Map + interaction design
  • Data QA + spatial validation
  • Visual system decisions (hierarchy, labels, color)
  • Prototyping & communication
Key design decisions
  • Hierarchy: muted context so data leads
  • Accessibility: color ramp legibility + contrast discipline
  • Declutter: simplified boundaries to reduce noise
  • Progressive detail: reveal complexity as users zoom
Outcome
  • Improved interpretability for non-technical audiences
  • Clear visual priority across zoom levels
  • Reusable mapping pattern for other risk datasets
Clarity over density Scale-aware design Accessible cartography Human-centered mapping

My approach blends cartographic craft (hierarchy, symbolization, scale) with UX principles (progressive disclosure, clear affordances, and usability). I care about how people interpret spatial information — not just how it looks.
