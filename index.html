Flagship case study

Design thinking + spatial decisions

Flood Risk Visualization for Urban Decision-Making

Urban planners needed a fast, intuitive way to explore where flood risk overlaps with vulnerable populations. I designed a map-based interface that prioritizes legibility, scale-aware detail, and interpretability.

Key decisions included muted contextual basemaps, accessible color ramps, simplified boundaries, and interaction patterns that reveal detail progressively as users zoom and explore.

How I approach map design

I treat maps as product interfaces, not illustrations. My approach emphasizes clarity over density, accessibility over decoration, and interaction patterns that support human decision-making. I care deeply about how users interpret spatial information — not just how it looks.
